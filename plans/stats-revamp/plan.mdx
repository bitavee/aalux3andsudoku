<RichText id="b-objective">

### Objective & done criteria

Today AALU's Stats screen shows two numbers (lifetime reading time and finished-book count) plus a short book list. This revamp turns it into a **motivation engine**: a reading streak you do not want to break, a daily goal with visible progress, a calendar heatmap, a Year-in-Review, sharper per-book insight, and a **Bookworm Cat** companion whose mood tracks your habit.

**Done** means: a reader who opens Stats sees their current streak, today's goal progress, and the cat's mood at a glance; the formerly invisible Reading/Finished toggle is a labelled tab; the per-book screen renders correctly in every orientation; and all of it rides one `stats.bin` v7 migration so later screens are pure UI fast-follows with no second format bump.

</RichText>

<Callout id="b-recommend" tone="decision">

**Recommended shape: ship the full v7 data model now, then layer the UI in phases.** The migration is the only irreversible step, so pay it once — the full 365-day history plus streak, goal, achievement, pet, and speed fields — even though the first UI cut renders only some of it. Then the heatmap, Year-in-Review, achievements grid, and per-book sparkline become UI-only additions with **no second migration**. RAM (+951 B, 0.24% of the 380 KB ceiling) and flash (~1.78 MB headroom, verified) are non-issues; the real constraints are clock-correctness and how many new screens you harden at once.

</Callout>

<RichText id="b-canvas-pointer">

### What you are looking at

The six screens above are the proposed Stats surfaces in portrait (the X4's reading orientation). **Overview** is the hero — streak, today's goal, and the cat lead the page. **Calendar** is the reading heatmap, **Awards** the achievements grid, **Year in Review** the annual recap, and the per-book screen is the redesigned `DetailedStatsActivity`. Every screen reflows to landscape from `renderer.getScreenWidth()/getScreenHeight()` — no hardcoded 800/480. A few decisions are still open — see **Open Questions** at the bottom.

</RichText>

### Data model: stats.bin v7

Everything rides one new on-disk format, `stats.bin` v7 (today's is v6 in `src/stats/ReadingStatsManager.{h,cpp}`). The v7 structs keep v6's **exact byte prefix**, so migration is a pure tail-append: read the old bytes, zero the new fields, save as v7, and re-evaluate achievements once so long-time readers immediately earn the hours/books they already have. The centerpiece is a **365-entry ring of daily reading minutes** in `GlobalStats`; streak, goal, achievement, pet, and speed values are cheap scalars layered on top. Per-book time-to-finish and reading-speed read existing fields plus a tiny 4-sample speed ring.

</RichText>

<Callout id="b-v7-budget" tone="info">

**Total: +951 B RAM** — `GlobalStats` 44 → 804 B, `BookStatEntry` 468 → 488 B (x9 books) — all static, zero malloc, **0.24% of the 380 KB ceiling**. `goalType`, `petStage`, `speedHead`, and `speedCount` reuse the v6 `_pad` bytes (0 net). On disk, `stats.bin` grows by the same ~951 B in one debounced SD write. The v4/v5/v6 → v7 load chain must stay intact (`ReadingStatsManager::load`), and the migration must zero `achievementBits` so existing users do not boot with phantom unlocks.

</Callout>

<RichText id="b-clock-head">

### The load-bearing risk: no real-time clock

</RichText>

<Callout id="b-clock-risk" tone="risk">

**The X4 has no RTC chip** (verified: `HalClock.h:24-25`, `HalClock.cpp:55`). `isAvailable()` is true only after a WiFi/NTP sync sets `time() >= 1700000000`, which then **persists across deep-sleep**. So streak, heatmap, daily goal, cat mood, and the today/week/month/year totals are meaningless until the reader syncs time **once**. The plan: (1) gate every per-day write on `isAvailable()`; (2) when clock-less, render a calm "Connect WiFi to start tracking your reading days" state instead of fake data; (3) survive an NTP correction (clock jumps) without zeroing valid history; (4) clock-less devices still get every lifetime total they have today. AALU already ships `syncFromNTP()` and `getTime()`, so the plumbing exists — this is a UX/onboarding task, not new infrastructure.

</Callout>

<RichText id="b-ia-head">

### Navigation & information architecture

Fold the new surfaces into one **tabbed** `StatsActivity`, reusing `GUI.drawTabBar` (already proven in `SettingsActivity`) so the invisible Reading/Finished toggle becomes a labelled tab. One grammar everywhere: **Left/Right** switch sibling tabs, **Up/Down** move within content, **Confirm** drills in (long-press removes a book), **Back** pops one level. Two new `ButtonHintGlyphSet` variants are added procedurally in `BaseTheme.cpp` — `StatsTabbed` (◀ ● ◁ ▷) and `GoalStepper` (◀ ● − +) — never the legacy rectangle hint bar. The glyph band itself now takes a `slotMask` so a screen renders only its active buttons in their fixed slots — already implemented in `BaseTheme::drawButtonHintsGlyphs` and applied to the per-book detail screen, which previously showed inert up/down arrows.

</RichText>

<RichText id="b-competitive-head">

### Competitive validation

</RichText>

<Callout id="b-competitive" tone="info">

AALU already out-features its on-device peers — CrossPoint upstream ships **zero** stats, CrossInk is minimal — so streaks + daily history make AALU the stats leader of the whole Xteink ecosystem. The design borrows the proven patterns: **KOReader** (time-to-finish, calendar), **Boox** (weekly chart), **Kindle/StoryGraph** (streaks, ahead/behind goal), **Goodreads** (Year in Books). Correctness lessons baked in: **forgiving** streaks ("X of the last 7 days", not a brittle daily chain), **idle-gap exclusion + page-dwell caps** so time and pace stay honest (the device deep-sleeps between page turns), **pages/hour never WPM** (EPUB has no on-device word index), a **stats-reset path** (Kobo became infamous for corrupted stats with no reset), and **numbers + a rule-based archetype** for Wrapped — never AI copy (Fable's AI Wrapped was widely panned).

</Callout>
